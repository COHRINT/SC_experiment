Your truck can report "Solver Quality" to help you

Solver Quality represents the truck's assessment of its ability to come up with a good plan. This is based on experience with having made plans in other similar conditions. It can be 'very bad', 'bad', 'okay', 'good', or 'very good'. A 'very bad' Solver Quality indicates that the truck does not believe it can make a good plan in a given situation. A 'good' Solver Quality indicates that the truck has made good plans in similar circumstances. A 'very good' Solver Quality indicates that the truck will be able to exceed its best plans made in similar conditions. For example:

  • In cities where the truck knows the road map is very poor it will be unconfident in it's capability and report a 'very bad' Solver Quality
  • In cities where there have been many well-maintained roads the vehicle will likely be confident in its capability and report a 'good' Solver Quality
  • In a city with a very accurate map, and little traffic the truck would likely report a 'very good' Solver Quality
Solver Quality does not indicate whether the outcome will be good or bad, but only the capability of making the best possible plan. Even the best plans can fail in a losing situation.
